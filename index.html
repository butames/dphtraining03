Reminder of Study Aim/ Objectives

Aim

A systematic review to investigate the effects of pharmacist-led CMM interventions on health-related outcomes (i.e., therapeutic, safety, and humanistic) and on economic outcomes in the United States.

Objectives

  1. Understand extent of CMM use
    2.  
  2. Understand the effectiveness of CMM interventions
    3.  
  3. Understand the costs associated with CMM
    4.  
  4. Guide our own policy work
 

PICO Framework

  • Help structure the research question.
  • Capture key elements of the process.
   
Constructs Definition/Meaning
Population/ patients Unique characteristics/ demographics
Intervention Specifics of the intervention
Comparator Standard of care/ another intervention
Outcome Intervention effects on population
We developed the systematic search using the PICO Framework. Population, Intervention, Comparator, Outcomes. The PICO can serve as a good tool when considering materials during your screening. It is helpful if you keep this in the back of your mind as you screen.

Include or Exclude -- The challenge of screening

 

At present we are worried about what to include and what to leave out.

Criteria to help

 
  • Standards/ criteria
    •  
    • Theoretical information
      •  
    • Practical needs

The Goal

 
  • The goal at this point is to efficiently sort what needs to be included.

What are the Inclusion Criteria?

 
  • Must be a CMM/ CMM-like
    •  
  • Must involve hypertensives/ pharmacists
    •  
  • Must have at least one of four outcome categries
       
    • Therapeutic, Safety, Humanistic, Economic
  • Must be a specific type of study (i.e., experimental/ observational)
    •  
  • Must be set in the USA
    •  
  • Must be in English
    •  
  • Must be published, 2010 - Present

Criteria 01 - CMM Intervention

 

Less details in title/ abstract

 
  • Titles are breif and catchy
    •  
  • Abstracts subjectively summarize study details
 

A mix of novelty and common terms

 
  • Comprehensive medication management (CMM)
    •  
  • Medication management
    •  
  • Medication therapy management (MTM)
    •  
  • Provider (i.e., pharmacist) collabs

Criteria 02 - Patient [or Provider] Population

 

The study has two populations (making things confusing).

  1. People with hypertension/ risk for hypertension
    2.  
  2. Pharmacists or pharmacists providing care to hypertensives

Criteria 03 - Study Outcomes

 
  • Therapeutic Outcomes
    •  
  • Safety Outcomes
 
  • Humanistic Outcomes
    •  
  • Economic Outcomes
